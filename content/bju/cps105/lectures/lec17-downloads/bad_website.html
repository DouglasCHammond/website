Nabokov's Blues Threatened

Vladimir Nobokov, the famous author or Lolita was also a lepidopterist. He studied and named butterflies known as Blues. He was interested in butterflies both scientifically and literalily. In his novel Pnin Nobokov wrote "A score of small butterflies, all of one kind, were settled on a damp patch of sand, their wing erect and closed, showing their pale undersides with dark dots and tiny orange-rimmed peacock spots along their hind-wing margins; one of Pnin's shed rubbers disturbed some of them and revealing the celestial hue of their upper surface, they fluttered around like blue snowflakes before settling again." This butterfly, Karner's Blue, named by Nobokov, is among several thousand threatened by extinction.
